2024 Color Trends

The most popular color palettes transforming spaces this year

Biophilic Colors

Nature continues to inspire interior design with earthy greens, warm browns, and soft blues that create calming, organic spaces. These colors help bring the outdoors in and establish a sense of tranquility in any room.

Perfect for living rooms, bedrooms, and home offices where you want to create a peaceful retreat from the modern world.

Recommended Color Palette:

Sage Green
Warm Taupe
Misty Blue
Golden Wheat
Forest Green

Best Pairings:

  • Pair sage greens with natural wood tones
  • Combine misty blues with crisp whites
  • Use forest green as an accent with neutral walls

Warm Neutrals

Cool grays are giving way to warmer beiges, terracottas, and creamy whites that create cozy, inviting atmospheres. These hues work beautifully in any room and pair well with both modern and traditional decor.

Ideal for creating a welcoming foundation throughout your home that allows for colorful accents and artwork to shine.

Recommended Color Palette:

Cream
Terracotta
Warm Taupe
Linen
Caramel

Application Tips:

  • Use lighter shades to make small rooms feel larger
  • Darker warm neutrals add sophistication to dining rooms
  • Pair with black accents for modern contrast
Get Color Consultation

Bold Accent Colors

While neutral bases remain popular, bold accent colors are making statements in unexpected ways. Deep blues, moody greens, and rich burgundies add drama and personality when used strategically.

Great for feature walls, cabinetry, front doors, and other architectural elements you want to highlight.

Recommended Color Palette:

Navy Blue
Moody Green
Burgundy
Mauve
Burnt Orange

How to Use Bold Colors:

  • Paint interior doors for unexpected pops of color
  • Use on kitchen islands or lower cabinets
  • Create an accent wall behind beds or sofas
  • Paint ceilings for a dramatic effect

Application Trends

Innovative techniques transforming how we use color

Color Drenching

This bold technique involves painting walls, trim, ceilings and even doors the same color for a cohesive, immersive effect. It creates visual continuity and makes spaces feel larger and more intentional.

Particularly effective in small spaces, powder rooms, and bedrooms where you want to create a cocoon-like atmosphere.

Best Colors for Drenching:

Drenched Blue
Drenched Mauve
Drenched Green
Drenched Sage
Drenched Navy

Technique Examples:

Living Room

Create a cozy, intimate space by drenching all surfaces in a warm neutral

Bedroom

Drench in soothing blues or greens for a restful retreat

Bathroom

Small bathrooms gain drama when fully drenched in color

Two-Tone Walls

Creative use of color blocking with horizontal, vertical, or curved dividers between two complementary colors. This adds visual interest and can help define different areas in open-concept spaces.

Works particularly well in living rooms, dining areas, and children's bedrooms where you want to create distinct zones.

Popular Color Combinations:

Blue & Orange
Green & Cream
Terracotta & Green
Mauve & Gold
Navy & White

Application Ideas:

  • Horizontal divide at chair rail height
  • Vertical split to define room areas
  • Curved lines for a playful, organic feel
  • Geometric shapes for modern spaces

Textured Finishes

From lime wash to Venetian plaster, textured finishes add depth and character to walls. These techniques create movement and visual interest that flat paint can't achieve.

Perfect for feature walls, fireplace surrounds, and entryways where you want to make a statement.
